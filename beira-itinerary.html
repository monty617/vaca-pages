"The Beiras are the Portugal that Portugal forgot to advertise — and that is precisely the point."

· · ·

This is interior Portugal at its most authentic: granite villages where people still live as their grandparents did, fortress towns built to guard a kingdom's borders, and mountain landscapes that feel like the edge of the world. No beaches, no tourist crowds. Just stone, history, and extraordinary food.

This three-day loop starts and ends in Penalva do Castelo, covering the very best of Beira Alta and Beira Baixa — designed to be unhurried, flexible, and rich in the kind of moments that make travel unforgettable.

Day One

Into the Mountains

A full, unhurried day in Portugal's highest mountain range — glacial valleys, wildflower plateaus, and a summit with views across two provinces.

Loriga→ Zêzere Valley→ Covão d'Ametade→ Torre Summit→ Belmonte
9:00 am
Loriga
Historic Village · Serra da Estrela · Gateway to the Glacial Valley

A local favourite and the perfect introduction to Serra da Estrela. Loriga sits at the mouth of its own glacial valley, with cobbled streets, a natural river beach, and a quieter, more intimate feel than the better-known mountain stops. It was here that the first Portuguese aviators completed their transatlantic crossing in 1922, and a small monument commemorates the feat. A gentle hour here — coffee, a walk, a dip in the river if the group is game — sets the tone beautifully for the day ahead.

⏱ Allow 1 hour 🏊 Natural river beach for a swim 🚶 Flat village centre, easy walk ☕ Coffee stop before the mountains Free entry
10:30 am
Glacial Valley of Zêzere
Natural Landmark · Serra da Estrela · ★ 4.8

Start here, and start early. The Zêzere glacial valley is the most dramatic landscape in all of Serra da Estrela — a classic U-shaped valley carved by ice over thousands of years, its flanks draped in wildflowers throughout July. The drive down from the high plateau toward the town of Manteigas is one of the great scenic roads in Portugal: pull over often. There's a well-marked hiking trail along the valley floor, and the river is cold and clear enough for a swim.

🏃 Teens: hike the valley trail or swim 🚗 Fully enjoyable from the car ⏱ Allow 90 min Free entry
12:30 pm
Covão d'Ametade
Glacial Lagoon · Serra da Estrela · ★ 4.7 (4,100+ reviews)

A short drive from the valley, Covão d'Ametade is a glacial lagoon tucked between granite cliffs and ancient pine forest — one of those places that seems too beautiful to be real. The water is an eerie, luminous green. Short walking paths ring the lagoon, making it an ideal picnic spot. Note: the access road from Manteigas is single-lane with traffic control stops in summer — budget an extra 30 minutes.

⏱ Allow 75 min 🅿️ Parking fills fast — arrive early Free entry 🧺 Bring a picnic — no facilities on site
2:00 pm
Torre — The Summit
Highest Point in Portugal · 1,993m

Drive the N339 all the way to the top of Torre, Portugal's highest point. Even in July, bring a layer — it's noticeably cooler up here. On a clear day you can see deep into Spain to the east and toward the Atlantic haze in the west. There's a small cluster of cafés at the summit — a good moment for lunch before descending toward Belmonte.

🧥 Bring a layer — cooler at altitude 🍽️ Lunch at summit café 📸 Standing at Portugal's highest point Free to visit
5:00 pm
Castelo de Belmonte
Medieval Castle · Belmonte · ★ 4.4 (5,100+ reviews)

About 45 minutes east of Torre, Belmonte is the birthplace of Pedro Álvares Cabral, the explorer who discovered Brazil in 1500. The medieval castle sits high above the town with commanding views across the Cova da Beira plains. An hour is plenty — pair it with a wander through the old village streets before checking in for the evening.

⏱ Allow 60–75 min ⚠️ Closed Mondays · 9am–12:30pm & 2–5:30pm 🏰 Hilltop castle with panoramic views 🚶 Compact and walkable
🌙
Night One — Belmonte
Pousada Convento de Belmonte

A former 16th-century convent converted into one of Portugal's most atmospheric hotels. Stone corridors, vaulted ceilings, hilltop views — and a legendary 8-course dinner showcasing wild boar, regional cheese, and local wine pairings. Waking up in a centuries-old monastery is something the whole group will remember for years. Rated 4.7 ★ (900+ reviews).

Budget alternative: Hotel Belsol, Belmonte ★4.3 — comfortable, good pool, excellent breakfast.

Day Two

Villages Out of Time

From houses wedged between boulders to a village with 2,000 years of layered history — ending the night inside Sortelha's ancient walls.

Monsanto→ Idanha-a-Velha→ Sortelha
9:30 am
Sortelha
Medieval Walled Village · Beira Baixa · ★ 4.8 (5,500+ reviews)

Sortelha is, by any measure, one of the most extraordinary places in Portugal. A medieval granite village perched on a hilltop, encircled by ancient walls with a castle at its heart — and people still live here, going about their lives surrounded by 800 years of stone. Walk the full circuit of the castle walls for panoramic views, then lose yourself in the narrow cobblestone streets. Arrive before 10am to have it largely to yourself. About 40 minutes from Belmonte.

⏱ Allow 2–2.5 hours 🕘 Arrive early before day-trippers 🏰 Walk the full castle wall circuit 🚶 Village centre flat; castle walls have steps Free entry to village
12:30 pm
Monsanto
Historic Village · ★ 4.8 · Your Home for the Night

Monsanto defies easy description. Once voted the "most Portuguese village in Portugal," the houses are built between colossal boulders — wedged into gaps in the rock, with granite outcroppings serving as walls, roofs, and foundations. Walking through it feels like stepping into a fantasy novel. Arrive for lunch, explore through the afternoon, and settle in for the night — because this village after dark, once the day visitors leave, is completely magical.

⏱ Afternoon + overnight 🍽️ Lunch in the village square 🪨 Exploring between giant boulders ☀️ Hot in July — sun protection essential Free entry
3:30 pm
Idanha-a-Velha
Ancient Village · Roman & Visigothic · ★ 4.5

One of the most ancient continuously inhabited places in Portugal — a tiny living village with 2,000 years of layered history in the same handful of streets: a Roman bridge, the finest Visigothic cathedral on the Iberian Peninsula, a Templar castle keep, and Moorish walls. All free, all walkable in 90 minutes, and almost eerily quiet. The tourist office staff are wonderfully helpful. Just 20 minutes from Monsanto — a perfect late-afternoon excursion before returning for the evening.

⏱ Allow 90 min 🚗 20 min from Monsanto Completely free ⚔️ Roman bridge & Templar ruins 🚶 Fully flat and walkable
🌙
Night Two — Sortelha
O Cantinho da Ana

A wonderfully cosy and authentic guesthouse right inside Sortelha's medieval walls, hosted by the warm and attentive Ana who goes above and beyond for her guests. Reviewers rave about the breakfasts and the deeply personal hospitality. After the day-trippers leave, Sortelha at dusk — completely silent, lit only by lantern light — is one of those travel moments that stays with you forever. Rated 4.7 ★ (84 reviews). +351 917 853 784.

⚠️ Very small property — book well in advance for July.

Day Three

Fortresses at the Edge of the Kingdom

A morning inside Sortelha's walls before the road north to Almeida — one of Europe's most perfectly preserved star fortresses — then home.

Sortelha→ Almeida→ Penalva do Castelo
11:30 am
Almeida
Star Fortress · Beira Alta · Near Spanish Border · ★ 4.6 (2,700+ reviews)

About 1h15 from Monsanto, Almeida is unlike anywhere else in Portugal. An 18th-century star-shaped military fortress — the most perfectly preserved of its kind in the country — with sharp angular bastions designed to deflect cannon fire. Walking the full perimeter takes about an hour and the star geometry, seen from the ramparts, is extraordinary. Real people still live inside: houses, a church, a square, and restaurants all within the walls. Have lunch at the celebrated Restaurante 1810, which reviewers consistently praise for outstanding traditional food at remarkable value — around €10 for a full meal.

⏱ Allow 2.5–3 hours 🚗 ~1h from Sortelha 🍽️ Lunch: Restaurante 1810 — ~€10/person ⭐ Walk the full star-shaped perimeter Free to enter the fortress
9:30 am
Sortelha — Morning in the Walls
Medieval Walled Village · ★ 4.8 · Your Base Last Night

After breakfast with Ana, take the morning to experience Sortelha at its absolute best — completely yours before the day visitors arrive. Walk the full castle wall circuit with fresh eyes, watch the granite glow in the morning light, and linger over the views toward Serra da Estrela. A gentle, unhurried start before heading north to Almeida.

⏱ Allow 1.5 hours 🚗 ~1h to Almeida from here 🏰 Castle walls at their quietest 🚶 Flat village centre, gentle pace Free entry
3:30 pm
Penalva do Castelo
Return Home · ~1h15 from Almeida

About 1h15 southwest from Almeida through the rolling Beira Alta countryside — a gentle, fitting close to three extraordinary days in one of Portugal's most rewarding and undervisited regions.

🚗 ~1h15 from Almeida

🚗
Getting Around

A car is essential — public transport does not serve these villages. Roads are generally good but mountain roads in Serra da Estrela can be narrow and slow. Allow extra time for the Covão d'Ametade access road, which has one-lane traffic control in summer. Day 3 has the longest morning drive: ~1h15 from Monsanto to Almeida.
